Sabah is a Malaysian state located on the northern part of Borneo, the third-largest island in the world. Bordered by Sarawak to the southwest and Kalimantan (Indonesia) to the south, Sabah faces the South China Sea on its west coast and the Sulu and Celebes Seas on the east. Known as the "Land Below the Wind" for its location just south of the typhoon belt, Sabah is a land of geographic and ecological diversity. The cultural fabric of Sabah is vibrant and deeply rooted in its more than 30 indigenous groups. Among them are the Kadazan-Dusun, Bajau, Murut, Rungus, and many others, each preserving their own traditions, languages, and customs. This cultural richness is celebrated in colorful festivals such as Kaamatan, the harvest festival, where music, dance, and food bring communities together. Everyday life in Sabah reflects a peaceful coexistence of various ethnicities and religions. Sabah’s natural beauty is one of its most defining features. Towering above it all is Gunung Kinabalu, Malaysia’s highest peak and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Lush rainforests, pristine rivers, exotic wildlife, and some of the world’s richest marine biodiversity make Sabah a paradise for nature lovers. Areas like Danum Valley, Kinabatangan River, and Sipadan Island are internationally renowned for conservation and ecotourism.
